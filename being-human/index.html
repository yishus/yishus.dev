Being Human

4 May 2021

I used to grow up spending a lot of time in front of the tv watching television dramas, and I used to justify that as my window to the human condition - I understood how to be human by watching other human make believe.

And then slowly I watched less and less, not sure if it's because we no longer make quality tv or that I just got tired of it. Recently I re-watched several early 2000s Japanese dramas and got reminded why I liked them so much. I don't read fiction much, and tv was my source of fiction. It just felt good seeing people overcome difficulties, learned to collaborate, and just love life.

It hit me to keep a list of content that reminds me of being human, And here it is

  • The Social Network. Without unpacking what this film means for us, look at them, human struggling to be human.
  • Jiro Dreams of Sushi. Also,
  • Chef's Table. Come on, this is a treasure trove.
  • Alex Honnold. He is a joy to watch, especially here.
